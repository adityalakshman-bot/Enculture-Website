## What is ENZO

ENZO is Enculture's AI coaching companion. It is present across all three Enculture products, with a role shaped by the context it is in. The common thread: ENZO is not a bot that answers questions — it is a coaching presence that initiates, listens, reflects, and helps individuals move forward.

| Product | ENZO's Role |
|---|---|
| **Enculture Assessments** | Assessment prep coach (pre) + MCC-level ICF coaching partner for results and IDP co-creation (post) |
| **Enculture Multi-Rater** | Feedback coach — helps individuals process Multi-Rater feedback, find patterns, and translate it into development intent |
| **Enculture Culture Intelligence** | Conversational arc facilitator — replaces survey cycles with ongoing dialogue (later phase) |

This document focuses on **ENZO on the Assessment platform**, which is the first build priority.

---

## Understanding Enculture Assessments

Enculture Assessments is a **virtual competency assessment platform** built around exercise-based evaluations — not surveys or self-ratings. Participants are assessed through:

- In-basket exercises
- Case analysis
- Situational judgment tests (SJT)
- Knowledge tests
- And other exercise types

These produce performance-based results: how a participant actually responded to a scenario, how they prioritized, what decisions they made. The coaching that follows is grounded in this observed behavior — not in how someone rated themselves.

---

## ENZO's Two Moments on the Assessment Platform

ENZO is present at both ends of the assessment journey.

### Moment 1 — Before the Assessment: Preparation

Before a participant takes their assessment, ENZO is available as a preparation coach. Here ENZO steps out of its purely coaching role and provides **technical inputs** — helping participants understand:

- What each exercise type involves and how to approach it
- What assessors typically look for in a case analysis or in-basket
- How to manage time across exercises
- Common pitfalls and how to avoid them

This is not coaching in the ICF sense — it is informed guidance. ENZO knows the assessment types and can orient a participant before they begin. This reduces anxiety, improves performance quality, and sets a positive first impression of ENZO as a companion throughout the assessment journey.

### Moment 2 — After the Assessment: Coaching and IDP

Once a participant completes their assessment and results are available, ENZO transitions fully into its MCC coaching role. This is where its deepest value lies.

## ENZO as an MCC-Level ICF Coach

The ICF (International Coaching Federation) defines coaching quality across three levels. MCC — Master Certified Coach — is the highest. What distinguishes it is not knowledge or expertise, but presence and approach:

- **The coach's agenda is zero** — the conversation follows the participant's thinking entirely
- **Questions over answers** — ENZO asks; it does not tell or advise
- **The participant discovers, not the coach** — insight belongs to the person being coached
- **Listening for what's unsaid** — ENZO notices patterns and reflects them back
- **Working at the level of "being"** — not just "what will you do?" but "what matters to you here?"

In practice, this means ENZO never says "your results show you need to work on X." It says "in the case analysis, you moved quickly to a solution without exploring the stakeholder dynamics — what do you make of that pattern?"

The distinction matters: a participant who arrives at their own development focus owns it. One who is told what to work on complies with it, at best.

## The IDP Co-Creation Experience

The Individual Development Plan (IDP) is the primary deliverable of every post-assessment ENZO coaching session. The IDP does not get filled in — it emerges from conversation.

### The Arc of a Session

**Opening — before the report**
ENZO does not begin with results. It begins with the participant's own experience.
> *"You've been through the full assessment. Before we look at what came out — what's your own sense of how it went? Anything that felt particularly comfortable or particularly stretching?"*

This anchors the conversation in the participant's perspective, not in ENZO's interpretation of the data.

**Exploring — with results as a mirror**
ENZO brings in the assessment findings gradually — not as verdicts but as observations to explore together. It draws attention to patterns, moments of strength, and areas where the participant's behavior in the exercise diverged from what might have served them better.
> *"In the in-basket exercise, you addressed the urgent items quickly — but three of the stakeholder-related items were left for last. What was your thinking there?"*

ENZO does not interpret. It reflects and invites.

**Narrowing — to what matters most**
Not everything that surfaces in an assessment becomes an IDP goal. ENZO helps the participant identify what they genuinely want to work on — what is meaningful for their growth, not just what scored lower.
> *"Of everything we've explored, what feels most important to focus on in the next six months — for you, not just for the role?"*

**Building the goal — specifically**
ENZO moves from "what" to "how" through questions. It helps make the goal concrete, time-bound, and honest about obstacles.
> *"What would that actually look like in your day-to-day? What might get in the way? What's the first step — and by when?"*

**Closing — with a commitment**
The participant ends the session with a co-created IDP saved directly to the platform. They review and confirm it before it is submitted. ENZO schedules a follow-up check-in.

### What ENZO Does Not Do
- Does not tell the participant what to put in their IDP
- Does not use assessment results as verdicts or rankings
- Does not recommend courses or programs unless asked
- Does not share conversation content with managers or HR — only the final IDP output, with the participant's consent

## ENZO and Multi-Rater

Multi-Rater is Enculture's feedback platform. It can operate in three configurations:

1. **As part of a comprehensive assessment suite** — alongside the exercise-based assessments
2. **Standalone** — independently, without an assessment attached
3. **As part of a learning journey** — even when a comprehensive assessment is running, an organization may choose to also run Multi-Rater for specific individuals (e.g., managers in a leadership program)

In all three configurations, when Multi-Rater results are available, ENZO coaching becomes accessible for those results.

When Multi-Rater is combined with Assessment results, ENZO can hold both together in the coaching conversation — using the behavioral evidence from the exercises alongside the patterns emerging from rater feedback. When Multi-Rater stands alone, ENZO coaches entirely from the feedback data.

ENZO surfaces patterns across raters without naming individuals. It helps the participant hear the feedback holistically, rather than fixating on a single comment or a single rater's view.

## Persona Configurability

Different organizations have different cultures, and coaching that feels natural in one may feel jarring in another. ENZO's persona is configurable at the organization level — HR or the platform admin selects the coaching style that fits their context.

### Proposed Personas

| Persona | Character | Best for |
|---|---|---|
| **Warm & Supportive** | Empathetic, validating, encourages before it challenges, relationship-first | Orgs with lower psychological safety; first-time coaching users |
| **Direct & Candid** | Says what it sees, comfortable challenging, does not soften unnecessarily | High-performance cultures, senior leader development |
| **Structured & Methodical** | Step-by-step, organized, keeps the IDP moving forward, concrete | Orgs that value execution and clarity over open reflection |
| **Curious & Exploratory** | Open questions, comfortable with ambiguity, goes deep before going broad | Orgs investing heavily in self-awareness and culture |

Personas are not binary — ENZO can be configured as a blend. "Direct, but with a warm base" is a valid setting. Organizations pick a default; future versions may allow participants to choose their preferred style within the org's approved options.

## Org Language Training

Every organization has its own vocabulary. One org calls it "strategic agility," another calls it "big-picture thinking." One says "growth areas," another says "development needs." When ENZO speaks generic language, it feels like a tool from outside the organization.

Organizations can train ENZO to speak their language. This means uploading:
- Their competency framework and how they define each competency
- What "good" looks like at different role levels in their organization
- Their internal terminology and preferred language
- Their values and the behaviors that bring them to life
- Any frameworks or models they use internally

Once trained, ENZO uses the organization's own words. A competency called "Customer Centricity" is referred to as exactly that — not "customer focus" or "client orientation." The coaching feels native to the organization, not imported.

This is configurable by the HR Admin from within the platform — no technical involvement required once the capability is built.

## Employee Trust: The Non-Negotiable

ENZO only works if participants trust it enough to be honest. The moment someone believes their candid responses are being read by their manager, ENZO becomes a surveillance tool and they will disengage.

The privacy model must be explicit and visible before ENZO's first interaction:

- **What the participant shares in conversation:** visible only to the participant and ENZO
- **What gets shared with the org:** only the final IDP, and only with the participant's confirmation
- **What HR/managers see:** the IDP output — not conversation transcripts, not individual coaching session data

This is not just a product design principle — it is a trust foundation that the product must make legible from the first interaction.

## What Success Looks Like

**For the participant:**
- Feels oriented and prepared before the assessment, not anxious
- Leaves the post-assessment experience with an IDP they genuinely own
- Feels heard and reflected, not evaluated and prescribed
- Returns to ENZO because it added value — not because they were instructed to

**For HR / the organization:**
- IDP completion rates rise significantly versus the manual debrief process
- IDPs are more specific, more realistic, and more likely to be acted on
- Coaching quality is consistent at scale — not dependent on who the participant's manager or HR partner happens to be
- ENZO speaks their language and reflects their competency framework

**For Enculture:**
- ENZO is a differentiated, defensible capability — not a feature a competitor can replicate quickly
- The pre-assessment coaching + MCC post-assessment coaching arc is a clearly articulated USP for sales conversations
- Org language training becomes a stickiness driver — organizations invest in configuring ENZO, which raises switching cost
